Maps, Location, and Cartographers

Norway's unique geographical position at the northern edge of Europe has made it a subject of fascination for cartographers throughout history. From ancient sea charts to modern satellite mapping, Norway's dramatic coastline and northern latitude present both challenges and innovations in cartographic representation.

Ancient Maps: The Carta Marina (1539)

This map, created by Olaus Magnus, is one of the earliest detailed maps of Scandinavia, particularly Norway. It shows medieval understanding of Norwegian coastlines, sea monsters, and settlements. The map reflects the maritime culture of Norway and the importance of sea routes for trade and exploration during this period (Magnus, 1539).

Trade Routes and Sea Voyages

Norway's strategic position along major European trade routes and its extensive coastline make it a crucial node for maritime commerce. The map shows modern shipping lanes that connect Norway to European markets, highlighting the country's role in North Sea oil transport and its historical importance for the Hanseatic League's trade networks (Norwegian Maritime Authority, 2023).

Continental Position and Latitudinal Range

Norway is located in Northern Europe, bordered by Sweden to the east, Finland and Russia to the northeast, and the Norwegian Sea, North Sea, and Atlantic Ocean to the west and south. The country spans latitudes 57°N to 71°N, encompassing the northernmost point of mainland Europe at the North Cape. This latitudinal range creates diverse climatic zones from temperate southern regions to Arctic conditions in the north (Central Intelligence Agency, 2024).

Modern Map Projection: UTM Zone 33N

This modern map uses the Universal Transverse Mercator (UTM) projection, specifically Zone 33N, which is optimal for Norway's east-west extent. UTM projections minimize distortion for regional mapping and are widely used in GPS systems and modern cartography for Norway's detailed topographic mapping (Norwegian Mapping Authority, 2023).

Boundary Changes Over Time

Norway's boundaries have remained relatively stable since the dissolution of the union with Sweden in 1905. However, the map shows the historical expansion under Charles XII (1697-1721) when Norway controlled territories including Jämtland and parts of modern Sweden. The modern borders established in 1920 have remained consistent, though the Svalbard archipelago remains under Norwegian sovereignty (Bjørkøe, 2014).

Key Questions About Norway

On which continent and in what general area is your country located?

Norway is located in Northern Europe, occupying the Scandinavian Peninsula and numerous smaller islands including Spitsbergen (Svalbard), Jan Mayen, and the Lofoten Islands. It forms part of the Nordic region, sharing the peninsula with Sweden to the east. Norway is bordered by the Arctic Ocean to the north, the Norwegian Sea to the west, and the North Sea to the south. The country extends from approximately 57°N in the southern region near Kristiansand to 71°N at the North Cape, making it one of the most northerly countries in the world (Central Intelligence Agency, 2024).

What is the latitudinal range of your country?

Norway's latitudinal range spans from approximately 57°38'N in the southern city of Kristiansand to 71°11'N at the North Cape (Nordkapp), the northernmost point of mainland Europe. This extensive north-south span of roughly 1,350 kilometers creates diverse climatic conditions: the southern regions experience temperate maritime conditions typical of western Europe, while the northern regions above the Arctic Circle experience polar night in winter and midnight sun in summer (Norwegian Meteorological Institute, 2023).

During what time periods or years has your country existed?

Norway as an independent nation has existed in several distinct periods. From 1030 to 1397, Norway was a powerful medieval kingdom. The Kalmar Union (1397-1523) united Norway with Denmark and Sweden under one monarch. The Danish-Norwegian Union lasted from 1524 to 1814, when Norway was ceded to Sweden. The Swedish-Norwegian Union existed from 1814-1905. Norway has existed as an independent constitutional monarchy since June 1905, when the Norwegian parliament declared dissolution of the union with Sweden on June 7, 1905 (Official website of the Norwegian government, 2023).

Famous Cartographers

Olaus Magnus (1480-1541) was a Swedish cartographer of Norwegian descent who created the famous Carta Marina (1539), one of the earliest detailed maps of Scandinavia and northern Germany. Exiled from Sweden after the Danish conquest, he spent years in Italy creating this groundbreaking map that showed Norwegian coastlines with unprecedented accuracy. His work was notable for including sea monsters and detailed information about Nordic culture alongside geographical features (Barsh, 1997).

Didrik Pining (c. 1530-1600) was a Norwegian cartographer and navigator who worked with the Dutch cartographer Petrus Plancius to create some of the first reasonably accurate maps of the Arctic regions. His work contributed to the understanding of northern sea routes and was influential in early Arctic exploration (Skelton, 1977).

The Norwegian Mapping Authority (Kartverket) founded in 1817, has been instrumental in modern Norwegian cartography. They developed the most accurate maps of the complex Norwegian coastline, including detailed nautical charts essential for navigation through Norway's intricate fjord systems (Norwegian Mapping Authority, 2023).
