Plate Tectonics, Earthquakes, and Volcanoes

This week's focus is on understanding Norway's position within global plate tectonics, its seismic activity, and volcanic history. While Norway is not known for active volcanoes today, its geological history reveals fascinating tectonic events.

Week 2 Research Topics

• Norway's tectonic setting: position relative to Eurasian and North American plates
• Ancient mountain-building events (Caledonian orogeny)
• Seismic activity in Norway: causes and frequency
• Volcanic rocks in Norway's geological record
• Post-glacial rebound and its effects on crustal stress
